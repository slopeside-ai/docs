## Why this checklist exists

Before activating your first audience, it’s important to confirm a few details that affect delivery, match rates, and learning.

Most issues seen by new users are caused by skipping one of these steps.

---

## Meta connection check

Confirm the following:

- The correct Meta Ads account is connected
- The connected user still has admin access
- The ad account is active and able to receive audiences
- No recent permission changes were made in Meta Business Manager

If anything has changed since connecting, reconnect Meta Ads before continuing.

---

## Audience readiness check

Before activating an audience:

- Confirm the audience matches your intended use case
- Avoid combining multiple audiences into a single activation initially
- Start with one clear audience per activation to give Meta clean signals

This helps Meta learn faster and avoids overlapping signals.

---

## Campaign planning check

You do not need to launch campaigns yet, but you should know:

- Which campaign or ad set the audience will be used in
- Whether the audience will be used directly or as a seed for lookalikes
- What creative or offer will be paired with the audience

Audience quality works best when creative and intent are aligned.

---

## Expectations to set internally

Before launch, align on a few expectations:

- Meta will still require a learning phase
- Performance may fluctuate early as Meta learns
- Slopeside improves inputs, not instant outcomes
- Creative and offer quality still matter

Setting these expectations early leads to better decision-making later.

---

## Ready to activate

If all of the above checks out, you’re ready to activate your first audience into Meta Ads.

From here, you can begin testing, learning, and scaling with stronger audience signals.
