Most Meta advertisers target audiences based on **who people are** — their age, location, interests, or demographic profile. Slopeside takes a different approach. It targets audiences based on **what people are actively doing** — their real-time behavioral signals.

This article explains what behavioral intent data is, why it outperforms traditional targeting, and how Slopeside uses it to improve your Meta Ads results.

## Interest Targeting vs. Behavioral Intent

Interest-based targeting (including Meta's own Advantage+ Audience) relies on static profile data: what pages someone has liked, what categories Meta has assigned them to, or broad demographic assumptions.

Behavioral intent data is different. It captures **signals of active, in-market behavior** — actions that indicate someone is currently researching, comparing, or ready to purchase in your category.

| | Interest Targeting | Behavioral Intent |
|---|---|---|
| **Data source** | Profile attributes | Real-time actions |
| **Freshness** | Static or slow-changing | Updated continuously |
| **Signal type** | Who someone is | What someone is doing right now |
| **Purchase proximity** | Often far from purchase | Close to purchase decision |

## What Behavioral Signals Does Slopeside Use?

Slopeside aggregates behavioral data from across the web — not just from Meta's ecosystem. This includes signals like:

- Visiting product or category pages in your niche
- Conducting research-style search behavior
- Engaging with comparison or review content
- Showing repeated interest in a specific product type over a short time window

These signals are combined and processed to identify people who are actively in-market — not just broadly interested.

<Note>
Slopeside does not use or store personally identifiable information (PII). All behavioral data is aggregated and privacy-safe before being used to build audiences.
</Note>

## Why Behavioral Intent Outperforms Interest Targeting

The core advantage is **purchase proximity**. Someone who visited three competitor websites, read two product reviews, and searched for pricing information in the last 48 hours is far more likely to convert than someone who "liked" a relevant Facebook page two years ago.

Behavioral intent data lets you reach the right people **at the right moment in their buying journey** — when they are actively looking and most receptive to your ads.

For Meta advertisers, this translates to:

- **Lower CPAs** — you're spending budget on people close to purchase, not broad audiences
- **Higher ROAS** — more of your spend reaches high-intent shoppers
- **Better audience efficiency** — smaller, more targeted audiences that outperform large broad audiences

## How Slopeside Builds Intent Audiences

Slopeside continuously processes behavioral signals and refreshes your custom audiences on a regular cadence. This means the people in your Slopeside audiences are always the ones showing active buying signals right now — not signals from weeks or months ago.

When you connect Slopeside to Meta, these audiences are pushed directly to your Meta Ads account as Custom Audiences and are ready to use in your campaigns immediately.
