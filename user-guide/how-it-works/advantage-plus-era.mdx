Meta has been pushing advertisers toward Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC) and Advantage+ Audience — its AI-driven, automated targeting systems. Many advertisers are asking: if Meta's AI is handling targeting automatically, does Slopeside still matter?

The short answer is yes. Here's why.

## What Advantage+ Does

Meta's Advantage+ systems use machine learning to automatically find the best audiences for your ads. They're effective at broad discovery and scale — Meta's algorithm is exceptionally good at finding people who are likely to engage with or purchase from your ads over time.

Advantage+ Audience removes manual targeting inputs and lets Meta's AI decide who to show your ads to based on pixel data, purchase history, and engagement signals.

## The Gap Advantage+ Doesn't Fill

Meta's AI optimizes based on **past conversion signals** — who has purchased from you before, who has visited your site, and who resembles those people. This is powerful for retargeting and broad prospecting.

What it doesn't have is **real-time external intent data** — signals from outside Meta's ecosystem indicating who is actively in-market right now.

Someone who has been researching your product category across the web for the past 48 hours, visiting competitor sites, reading reviews, and comparing prices is highly likely to convert. Meta's algorithm doesn't have visibility into those signals. Slopeside does.

<Note>
Meta only sees what happens on Meta. Slopeside adds external behavioral intent — what your best prospects are doing across the rest of the web.
</Note>

## How Slopeside and Advantage+ Work Together

Slopeside doesn't replace Advantage+ — it feeds it better inputs.

When you add a Slopeside behavioral intent audience as a Custom Audience within an Advantage+ campaign, you're giving Meta's algorithm a head start. Instead of discovering your best customers purely through trial and error, the algorithm starts with a pool of people who are already demonstrating active in-market behavior.

This works in several ways:

**As a seed audience for Advantage+ Audience** — Upload your Slopeside audience as a suggestion layer, giving Meta's AI a warm starting point rather than a cold start.

**As a Custom Audience in ASC** — In Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns, you can include Custom Audiences as an "existing customer" or warmup audience segment, ensuring your highest-intent prospects are included in the campaign reach.

**As a standalone audience in manual campaigns** — For advertisers who still run some manually targeted campaigns alongside ASC, Slopeside audiences can be used directly as the targeting pool.

## Testing Slopeside Within Advantage+ Campaigns

The recommended approach is to test Slopeside as an additional audience layer rather than replacing your existing ASC setup.

Run a parallel test:
- Campaign A: Your existing ASC setup (control)
- Campaign B: ASC with Slopeside audience included as a Custom Audience layer

Measure CPAs and ROAS over a 2–4 week window. Most advertisers see meaningful performance improvement when external intent audiences are added to their Advantage+ campaigns.

See [How to Run the Test](/user-guide/testing/run-the-test) for the full recommended test setup.

## The Bottom Line

Meta's AI is powerful, but it works with the data it has access to. Slopeside extends that data set by adding external behavioral intent signals that Meta simply cannot see on its own. The combination of Meta's optimization engine and Slopeside's in-market audience data is more powerful than either one alone.
