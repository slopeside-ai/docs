## How to judge test results

When evaluating a test, focus on trends and consistency rather than single-day results.

Meta’s learning phase can cause short-term fluctuations that are normal.

---

## Metrics to pay attention to

While exact metrics vary by business, common indicators include:
- Cost per conversion or lead
- Conversion rate
- Stability of performance over time
- Spend consistency

Compare these metrics between your baseline audience and the Slopeside audience.

---

## What “success” looks like

A successful test may show:
- Lower costs
- Improved consistency
- Faster stabilization
- Clear directional improvement

Not every test produces a dramatic win. Directional improvement is still meaningful.

---

## Interpreting mixed results

If results are mixed:
- Check that the test setup was clean
- Confirm enough time passed
- Avoid overreacting to small differences

It’s common for performance to improve gradually as Meta learns.

---

## Common questions

**What if results are flat?**  
Flat results still provide information. Consider testing a different audience or use case.

**What if Slopeside underperforms initially?**  
Initial underperformance does not always indicate failure. Review setup and allow sufficient learning time.

---

## What to do next

Once you’ve evaluated results, decide whether to scale, iterate, or test another audience.

If results were unclear, review **Mistakes** to identify possible issues.
