## The only honest way to evaluate audience quality

Meta Ads performance is influenced by creative, offer, landing page, bid strategy, seasonality, and budget — among other things. Audience quality is one input among many.

The only way to isolate the audience variable and know whether Slopeside is contributing to better performance is to run a clean, controlled test. Everything else stays the same. The audience is the only thing that changes.

This is not a Slopeside-specific requirement. It is how any responsible audience test should be run on Meta.

## What a valid test tells you

A properly structured test answers one specific question: does this audience produce a lower cost per acquisition than my current best audience, given identical creative, budget, and campaign conditions?

If the answer is yes, you have evidence that Slopeside is working in your account. If the answer is no or inconclusive, you have real data to work with — either to adjust your approach or to make a different decision.

A valid test also gives you something you can show your team, your client, or your leadership. Numbers from a clean A/B comparison are defensible in a way that gut feel or anecdotal observations are not.

## Why the control matters

Your control ad set needs conversion history. This is the most important structural requirement of the test.

If you create two new ad sets simultaneously — one Slopeside, one control — both enter Meta's learning phase at the same time. Both produce erratic, unreliable results for the first several days. You end up comparing two unstable systems against each other, which tells you nothing meaningful.

Using an existing ad set with conversion history as your control gives you a stable baseline. Meta's algorithm already understands that ad set. You are introducing one new variable — the Slopeside audience — and measuring its effect against a known reference point.

## Why 14 days

Meta's learning phase requires approximately 50 conversion events per ad set per week before delivery stabilizes. For most mid-market budgets, that takes several days at minimum. Performance in the first three to five days is often misleading — CPAs swing widely as Meta explores.

Fourteen days gives you two full weekly cycles, which matters particularly for B2B audiences where buying behavior follows weekday patterns. B2B Meta audiences tend to underperform on weekends and spike on Mondays. A 7-day window can catch a bad weekend and produce a false negative.

Two weeks is the minimum for a reliable read. More budget compresses the timeline — if you are spending enough to hit 50 conversions per ad set in a few days, your results will be reliable sooner.
