---
title: "Common Test Mistakes"
description: "The errors that most commonly produce bad or inconclusive test data."
---

These are the mistakes that invalidate a Slopeside test most often. Most of them are easy to avoid if you set the test up correctly from the start.

## Using a new ad set as your control

What happens: Both ad sets enter Meta's learning phase simultaneously. Results in the first week are unreliable for both, making comparison meaningless.

Fix: Use an existing ad set with conversion history as your control. Duplicate it and swap only the audience. See [Run the Test](/user-guide/testing/run-the-test).

## Editing the campaign during the test

What happens: Any change to budget, creative, audience, or campaign structure resets Meta's learning phase for the affected ad set. The comparison becomes invalid.

Fix: Set it up correctly on day one and do not touch it for 14 days. If you need to make changes to a live campaign for business reasons, pause the test first and restart it cleanly.

## Using CBO instead of ABO

What happens: Meta shifts budget toward whichever ad set shows early promise — often before either ad set has reliable data. The Slopeside ad set may receive significantly less spend than intended, producing an underpowered test.

Fix: Set budget at the ad set level using Ad Set Budget Optimization (ABO). Each ad set should receive a minimum of $50 per day independently.

## Judging results too early

What happens: CPA in the first three to five days is often misleading. Meta is still in its learning phase, delivery is erratic, and early results can look much worse — or better — than the final outcome.

Fix: Do not review or act on results until day 14. Set a calendar reminder and ignore the data until then.

## Pausing the Slopeside ad set when early CPA looks high

What happens: Early high CPA is normal during learning phase. Pausing resets learning and wastes the budget already spent. You restart from zero with less trial time remaining.

Fix: Expect higher CPA in the first few days. This is part of how Meta's learning phase works for any new ad set. Let it run.

## Testing audience and creative simultaneously

What happens: If you use different creative in the Slopeside ad set than in your control, any performance difference could be caused by the creative, not the audience. You cannot isolate the variable.

Fix: Identical creative in both ad sets. The only difference between your control and your Slopeside ad set should be the audience.

## Comparing against a retargeting ad set

What happens: Retargeting audiences (website visitors, existing customers, video viewers) convert at a fundamentally different rate than cold prospecting audiences. Comparing Slopeside — a prospecting audience — against a warm retargeting audience is not a fair test.

Fix: Your control should be your best cold prospecting ad set. Compare like for like.

## Not tagging ad sets in the Performance tab

What happens: Without tags, the Slopeside dashboard cannot pull the right data or display your comparison. You are running a test with no way to read the results in one place.

Fix: Tag your ad sets in the Performance tab as soon as the campaign is live. This takes two minutes and makes your results significantly easier to interpret.
