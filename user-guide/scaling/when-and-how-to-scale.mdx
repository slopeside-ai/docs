title: "When and How to Scale"
description: "How to recognize when your Slopeside audiences are ready to scale, and the right approach to increasing budget and reach."
---

Once you've validated Slopeside through an A/B test and confirmed it's outperforming your control audience, the next question is: how do you scale it?

This article covers the signals that indicate you're ready to scale, the right approach to increasing budget, and what to watch for as you grow.

## Signs You're Ready to Scale

Before increasing spend, confirm you have a clear performance signal. You're ready to scale when:

- Your Slopeside campaign has been running for at least 2–4 weeks
- You have statistically meaningful data (at minimum, 50+ conversions in the test period)
- Slopeside CPA is meaningfully lower than your control (typically 15%+ improvement)
- Your ROAS from the Slopeside campaign is above your target threshold
- Audience size is sufficient — your Slopeside Custom Audience should be at least 10,000–20,000 people for efficient delivery

<Note>
Don't scale based on early data. The first 7–10 days of a campaign are often the learning phase. Wait for the algorithm to stabilize before making budget decisions.
</Note>

## How to Scale Budget

When scaling, increase budget gradually rather than making large jumps. Large budget increases can force Meta's algorithm back into a learning phase, disrupting performance.

**Recommended scaling cadence:**
- Increase daily budget by no more than 20–30% at a time
- Wait 3–5 days after each increase before evaluating performance
- If CPA degrades by more than 20% after a budget increase, hold at current level for another week before increasing again

This gradual approach allows Meta's delivery system to adjust without triggering a full learning reset.

## Scaling Audience Reach

There are two ways to extend reach as you scale:

**Option 1: Add multiple Slopeside audience tiers**
If your Slopeside plan includes multiple audience segments (e.g., high-intent and mid-intent tiers), you can expand from your highest-intent audience to broader segments. Start with your tightest audience and expand outward as you scale.

**Option 2: Use Slopeside as a seed for Lookalike Audiences**
Your validated Slopeside Custom Audience can be used as the seed for a Meta Lookalike Audience. Because the seed is composed of high-intent behavioral signals rather than just past purchasers, the resulting lookalike tends to be higher quality than one built from a standard customer list.

To create a lookalike from a Slopeside audience:
1. Go to Meta Ads Manager → Audiences
2. Select your Slopeside Custom Audience
3. Create a 1% Lookalike Audience from it
4. Test the Lookalike as an additional ad set alongside your direct Slopeside audience

## What to Monitor as You Scale

As you increase budget and reach, watch these metrics closely:

- **CPA trend** — Some CPA increase is normal as you scale into larger audiences. A gradual increase of 10–20% is acceptable. A sharp increase signals you may be exhausting your highest-intent pool.
- **Audience overlap** — If you're running multiple Slopeside audiences alongside retargeting and other campaigns, check for audience overlap in Meta's Audience Insights. Significant overlap can inflate CPAs.
- **Frequency** — As budgets increase, frequency can rise quickly in smaller audiences. If frequency exceeds 3–4x per week, consider expanding your audience or adding fresh creative.
- **Audience refresh** — Slopeside audiences are updated regularly, but very high-spend campaigns can exhaust the current audience pool. See [Refreshing Your Audiences](/user-guide/scaling/refreshing-audiences) for guidance.

## When to Pull Back

Scaling isn't always linear. If you see CPA increase by more than 30% over a 7-day period after a budget increase, consider:

1. Holding budget flat for 1–2 weeks to let performance restabilize
2. Checking audience size — if the Custom Audience has shrunk significantly, the signal pool may be thinner
3. Adding a new creative rotation to combat fatigue
4. Contacting Slopeside support to review your audience configuration
