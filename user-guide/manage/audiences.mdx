## Audiences in Slopeside

Audiences are the foundation of how Slopeside works.

Each audience represents a group of people identified based on real research and evaluation behavior related to a specific topic, product, or intent.

Audiences are not static lists and they are not tied to your existing customers unless explicitly designed that way.

---

## What an audience represents

A Slopeside audience reflects:
- People actively researching or evaluating a topic
- Behavior observed across multiple signals
- Intent, not assumptions or demographics

Audiences are designed to be used as inputs for Meta Ads optimization.

---

## How audiences are created

Audiences are built using Slopeside’s underlying data models and refreshed over time.

Some audiences are:
- Available directly in the Catalog
- Requested by customers and approved for shared use
- Built privately for a specific customer

The way an audience is created determines how it can be used and who can access it.

---

## Are audiences shared?

It depends on the audience type:

- **Catalog audiences** are shared and available to all Slopeside customers
- **Requested audiences** may become shared if approved
- **Private custom audiences** are only accessible to your account

If an audience is private, no other customer can see or use it.

---

## Do audiences change over time?

Yes. Most audiences refresh on a regular basis to reflect current intent.

This means:
- New users may be added
- Users who no longer show intent may fall out
- Audience size can fluctuate naturally

This is expected behavior and helps keep signals relevant.

---

## Common questions

**Can I edit an audience?**  
No. Audiences are managed by Slopeside to ensure consistency and data quality.

**Can I delete an audience?**  
You can stop using an audience at any time. Removing it from campaigns does not affect others.
